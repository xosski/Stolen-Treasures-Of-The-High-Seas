A user authorized to access their own 23andMe profile can enumerate genetic and health-sharing connections via specific endpoints that expose sensitive personal, relational, and health-related data. This appears to result in information disclosure through accessible API responses, which may not be properly permission-scoped or access-controlled for the type or depth of data returned.
Affected Endpoints:
/tools/your-connections/connection/
/tools/your-connections/close-family/
/tools/your-connections/invitations/
The /connection/ endpoint returns full relationship listings with sensitive metadata (e.g., health sharing, sex, genotyping status, birth year, platform version). The /close-family/ endpoint leaks predicted familial relationships (e.g., "Aunt") along with full names and identifiers.
